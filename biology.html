Tuberculosis

This project is a mini report I did for my infectious disease Tuberculosis with information about how you contact the disease and how you can prevent the disease from happening to you.

Causes the infectious disease Tuberculosis (TB) is caused by bacteria Mycobacterium Tuberculosis that most often affect the lungs. Tuberculosis is curable and preventable. TB is spread from person to person through the air. When people with lung TB cough, sneeze or spit, they propel the TB germs into the air.

Transmitted through person contract the disease by a bacteria that spreads from one person to another person through microscopic droplets released into the air. This can happen when someone with the untreated active form of Tuberculosis coughs, speaks, sneeze, spits, laughs, and sings. A person needs to inhale only a few of these germs to become infected.

Positive TB skin test or TB blood test only tells that a person has been infected with TB bacteria. It does not tell whether the person has latent TB infection (LTBI) or has progressed to TB disease. Other tests, such as a chest x-ray and a sample of sputum, are needed to see whether the person has TB disease.

Ezcema This project I am doing research on my health issue and making a board game to teach bilingual elementary school students.

When a person has ezcema the affected area of their skin becomes red, very itchy, irritated and flaky. Ezcema can affect the entire body but is more commonly seen on certain body regions such as on the cheeks, face, elbow, knees, hands, arms, neck, eyelids, and wrists.
